Studio

Spatial thinking, applied to interface

PRISM was founded on a simple bet: the flat web is over. Every brief we take starts with a question — what does this idea look like in space, not just on a page?

We're a small team of WebGL engineers, motion designers, and art directors who obsess over frame timing as much as typography. The result is work that feels less like a website and more like an environment.

Capabilities

Everything a 3D site needs, none of the bloat

We build on a stack tuned for cinematic motion without sacrificing the metrics that matter — load time, accessibility, and search visibility.

WebGL scenes

Custom Three.js environments — particles, displaced geometry, and physically-based lighting tuned per project.

Scroll choreography

GSAP ScrollTrigger timelines that tie camera, light, and object transforms to scroll position with zero jank.

Glass interfaces

Layered, translucent UI that reacts to the scene behind it instead of sitting flat on top.

Mouse-reactive light

Pointer position drives real-time light rigs in the scene, so every scroll feels hand-tuned to the visitor.

Performance budget

Every scene ships with a frame budget. We lazy-load geometry and degrade gracefully on low-power devices.

Accessible by default

Reduced-motion fallbacks, semantic markup, and keyboard-navigable menus — depth without excluding anyone.
